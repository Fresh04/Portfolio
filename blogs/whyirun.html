Why I Run

Sep 2025 · ~4 min read
Running Health Journey

I started running in May during summer break of college. Sixth Semester of College was done. I had no internship for the summer. I was about ~100 kgs, and didn't feel good with myself — so I laced up. I started Running. It was hard at first. Couldn't run even 1km in a go. But ran every every day. My first 5km took about an hour and felt like a marathon. 25 straight days of running daily, my stamina grew. I could now run 5km without stopping and in about 35 minutes now. Lost about 7-8 kgs in the process. Now it wasn't just about health. A day without running felt sore. Felt like something was off. I started to enjoy the runs.

Kudos from Strava gave me a never before seen hits. Simply Running wasn't an option anymore. I started setting goals for PR times and distances. First sub 5 1K, first sub 30 5k. Somewhere in there I fell into a Casey Neistat rabbit hole. His love for running is infectious — the shots, the city, the grit. That energy kept me going. I would watch his videos of he broke the running barriers even after being in a life changing accident. His NYC marathon videos are a must watch.

We may be dying but we are not dead yet!!

Mid-June I got an onsite internship at Programming Pathshala. Running paused, but I stayed active: squats, push-ups, walking, and a calorie deficit. I'd watched Ryan Trahan for a while — didn't realize he was a D1 athelete. His running videos quietly raised the bar for me. His videos are genuinely so wholesome and engaging. Bro is living the life.

At the start of May I couldn't do one perfect push-up. By August, I could do twenty.

My internship ended on July 31. Back at college I started running again and began beating my PBs. College is busy but I try to find some time for running. Weight dropped to 84 kgs. I ran an unofficial half-marathon, then joined the Red Run 12K (CG Govt × NSS). Community miles hit different.

Current goal: Train for a full marathon — aiming for the TGCG Marathon on 7th Dec. The goal is too hard but where is the fun in achieving small goals.

Why I'm Sticking With It

  • Simple rules, honest feedback. You either ran or you didn't.
  • Mental clarity. Best ideas show up after kilometer five and it brings down a lot of mental pressure.
  • Identity shift. “Why not lose weight when you can?” turned into “Why not run when you can?”

Inspiration

Two Casey videos that pumped me up to get outside.
